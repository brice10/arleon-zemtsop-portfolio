Research Engineer

Inria · Université de Lille
📍 Lille, France
Oct 2024 — September 2025

Context & Mission

At Inria and the University of Lille, I worked in the field of autonomous systems and cloud computing. My research focused on self-adaptation of distributed systems in the cloud, with the goal of designing mechanisms enabling microservices to autonomously adapt to the dynamic conditions of their environment.

Key Achievements

  • AdaptiFlow: Design and development of a lightweight, extensible Java framework enabling microservices to autonomously adapt to dynamic cloud conditions. The framework relies on abstraction layers for the monitoring and execution phases of the MAPE-K cycle.
  • Adaptable TeaStore: Built a cloud-native benchmark platform dedicated to evaluating self-adaptive capabilities, validating concrete scenarios (database recovery, DDoS mitigation, traffic management).
  • Decentralised event-driven architectures: Engineering of event-driven pipelines to orchestrate the four self-management properties: self-healing, self-protection, self-optimisation, and self-configuration.
  • MAPE-K Abstraction: Development of metrics collectors and adaptation actions for cloud microservices, with systematic workflows minimising intrusion into existing code.
